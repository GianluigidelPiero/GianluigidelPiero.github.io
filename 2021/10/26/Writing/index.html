Why buy a house?

The ownership of home is of great significance in some countries. In these countries, owning a home rather than renting one is attached more importance. In this essay, I’m going to explore the reasons of this situation and give my own perspective.

Why is that case? The most obvious reason is that home is supposed to be exactly what it sounds like, a place for family to live in. A home is the container of feelings, memories and love related to family. For those who want to get married and build thier family, it’s neccessary to own a home because a home can be a shelter for their family and a place to recharge themselves after an exhausting day.

Admittedly, some people argue that home is not a necessity in our life. They think renting a home is acceptable and is much more reasonbale than buying one in some occasions. Take the financial crisis in 2008 as an example. Because of the bursting of United States housing bubble, millions of people lost their jobs and incomes. As a result, under the pressure of mortgages and the collapse of housing price, many people fled from their homes. But for those who only rented home, the damage could be not that severe.

However, I still prefer to buy my own home rather than rent one, and I consider this practice very positive and essential. The reason is rather realistic. Nowadays, housing is always closely tied to daily life that nobody can get away from, such as career planning, health care, children’s education, etc. Besides, people always like to be the one to decide what happens to the place they live in, but if you rent a home, painting a wall will even be impracticable without owners’ permission.

In conclusion(Bottom line), stable residence is crucial to the happiness of the whole family, and personally I would definately buy my house because then whatever happens is on me and no one else.

The financial crisis of 2007-2008

Predatory lending targeting low-income homebuyers, excessive risk-taking by global financial institutions, and the bursting of the United States housing bubble culminated(达到顶点 以…告终) in a “perfect storm”. MBS tied to American real estate, as well as a vast web of derivatives linked to those MBS, collapsed in value. Financial institutions worldwide suffered severe damage, reaching a climax with the bankruptcy of Lehman Brothers on September 15, 2008 and a subsequent international banking crisis.
